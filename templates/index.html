Late Blight is the most deadly and economically important disease of potato.

What to do next-

  • There is no effective treatment for Late Blight.
  • Remove and dispose effected plant immediately.
  • Remove and replace blight-infected garden soil.
  • The use of resistant cultivars and pathogen-free varaites of the crop are good preventions of disease

Early blight in potato is, along with mildew, one of the most significant diseases affecting this crop

What to do next-

  • If only a small number of leaves are affected, remove and dispose of them.
  • If the infection has spread, cut the foliage and stems.
  • Leave the crop for at least two weeks to let the blight spores on the surface die and the potatoes to develop a thicker skin.
  • The use of resistant cultivars and pathogen-free varaites of potao are good preventions of disease.
